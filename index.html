What I will be teaching

The curriculum is positioned around what professionals actually need to progress: system-level ownership, technical clarity, business framing, ambiguity handling, and promotion-ready thinking.

  • Week 1: TPM role clarity — moving from ceremonies to system-level ownership
  • Week 2: Technical depth — architecture literacy and distributed systems basics
  • Week 3: Ownership shift — problem definition and structured clarity from L3 to L4
  • Week 4: Cross-team execution — dependency mapping and stakeholder alignment from L4 to L5
  • Week 5: Roadmap structuring — sequencing initiatives under constraints
  • Week 6: GenAI for TPMs — AI-driven documentation, synthesis, and risk extraction
  • Week 7: Business framing — connecting technical initiatives to business impact
  • Week 8: Influence without authority — handling senior stakeholder conflict
  • Week 9: Ambiguity navigation — pre-mortems, risk modeling, and decision framing
  • Week 10: Scaling and launch — multi-region rollout planning
  • Week 11: L6 thinking — strategy definition, scope expansion, and leverage

Frequently asked questions

Is this only for people already working as TPMs?

No. The core audience includes aspiring Scrum Masters, Scrum Masters, Program Managers, and other experienced professionals transitioning toward stronger TPM-style roles.

Is this beginner-friendly?

It is beginner-friendly for agile and technical leadership concepts, but it is not a casual overview course. It is designed for people serious about building a stronger career path.

Will financial concepts and GenAI be covered too?

Yes. Foundational financial fluency and practical GenAI workflows are integrated into the program because modern TPMs need both business and leverage thinking.

What is the difference between Tier 1 and Tier 2?

Tier 1 includes the full 12-week core program. Tier 2 includes everything in Tier 1 plus advanced interview simulations, mock interviews, and personalized feedback.
